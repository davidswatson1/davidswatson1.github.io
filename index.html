Sample Work

Demand Forecasting | Summer 2023

View work on Google Sheets

In a Supply Chain Management course, I tackled a project focusing on data analysis and demand forecasting for a gas station. This project involved utilizing Excel to implement five distinct forecasting methods, comparing each method's estimates to actual results.

Learn More

Forecasting Methods Used

  1. Naïve Approach: The simplest of the five methods, it uses last month's demand to estimate the current month's demand.
  2. Three-Month Moving Average: This approach employs a rolling average over three months to forecast demand.
  3. Exponential Smoothing: Two variations of this method were employed, which is a widely-used statistical technique in demand forecasting. Exponential smoothing gives more weight to recent data while gradually reducing the significance of older data. Its flexibility allows businesses to fine-tune how quickly historical data diminishes in importance, making it a valuable tool for precise and adaptive predictions.
  4. Seasonality Index: The final method involved tracking cyclic patterns in demand. A seasonality index helps identify when demand is expected to rise or fall due to recurring factors like holidays or weather changes. For instance, in the gas station dataset, the index remains above 1.0 from April to September, peaking at approximately 1.15 in June and July. This indicates that demand for gas is highest during the warmer months, with June experiencing a 15% increase.

Takeaways: Through this project, I honed my skills in forecasting and data analysis, gaining valuable insights into predicting demand trends in a practical supply chain context.
