Sample Work

Strategic Proposal for Verizon - Financial Model | Spring 2024

View work on Google Sheets

In a senior strategic management course, my team had the unique opportunity to partner with Verizon Communications. This collaboration allowed us to engage directly with industry challenges and develop strategic proposals aimed at enhancing wireless service revenue. Our team's proposal was grounded in industry research and supported by a robust financial model, ensuring a realistic and viable strategy was presented. As the lead financial analyst for my team, I spearheaded the creation of a comprehensive discounted cash flow (DCF) valuation model. This model evaluated the projected financial outcomes of our targeted marketing campaign, which aimed to encourage recent college graduates to transition from family plans to their own service plans with Verizon.

Learn More

Financial Model - Key Components

  1. Revenue Projections: The model incorporated detailed revenue forecasts based on different user uptake scenarios for the proposed service plans. Revenue streams were diversified through various add-ons like device protection and international calling packages, with probabilities assigned to each based on market research.
  2. Cost Analysis: I conducted a thorough analysis of direct and indirect costs associated with the campaign, including promotional incentives like device payoff credits and partnership deals (i.e. Uber credits). Costs were amortized over the campaign's lifecycle to reflect real financial impacts accurately.
  3. Cash Flow Calculations: The model generated monthly and annual cash flow projections, taking into account the initial investment, ongoing operational costs, and incremental revenues. Each element was carefully calibrated to reflect potential market dynamics and customer behaviors.
  4. Discount Rates and Terminal Value: Utilizing Verizon's weighted average cost of capital (WACC) as the discount rate, I applied the DCF method to discount projected future cash flows to their present value. Additionally, the model included a terminal value calculated using a growing perpetuity method, adjusted by expected inflation rates, providing a comprehensive valuation of the campaign’s worth beyond the explicit forecast period.
  5. Sensitivity Analysis: To address potential uncertainties and risks, the model included a sensitivity analysis feature. This analysis evaluated how changes in key assumptions—such as churn rates, growth rates, and cost variables—impacted the overall valuation, enabling stakeholders to understand the potential range of outcomes.
  6. Performance Metrics: The model calculated essential financial metrics such as net present value (NPV), internal rate of return (IRR), and modified internal rate of return (MIRR), offering insights into the campaign’s profitability and economic viability.

Takeaways: My financial model was instrumental in our team's ability to articulate the economic value and strategic significance of our proposal. It provided a framework for discussing potential returns with Verizon's management and laid a solid foundation for strategic decision-making. The experience honed my analytical skills and deepened my understanding of how to apply financial modeling in real-world business scenarios.

Demand Forecasting | Summer 2023

View work on Google Sheets

In a Supply Chain Management course, I tackled a project focusing on data analysis and demand forecasting for a gas station. This project involved utilizing Excel to implement five distinct forecasting methods, comparing each method's estimates to actual results.

Learn More

Forecasting Methods Used

  1. Naïve Approach: The simplest of the five methods, it uses last month's demand to estimate the current month's demand.
  2. Three-Month Moving Average: This approach employs a rolling average over three months to forecast demand.
  3. Exponential Smoothing: Two variations of this method were employed, which is a widely-used statistical technique in demand forecasting. Exponential smoothing gives more weight to recent data while gradually reducing the significance of older data. Its flexibility allows businesses to fine-tune how quickly historical data diminishes in importance, making it a valuable tool for precise and adaptive predictions.
  4. Seasonality Index: The final method involved tracking cyclic patterns in demand. A seasonality index helps identify when demand is expected to rise or fall due to recurring factors like holidays or weather changes. For instance, in the gas station dataset, the index remains above 1.0 from April to September, peaking at approximately 1.15 in June and July. This indicates that demand for gas is highest during the warmer months, with June experiencing a 15% increase.

Takeaways: Through this project, I honed my skills in forecasting and data analysis, gaining valuable insights into predicting demand trends in a practical supply chain context.
